Engineering

Brief overview of the field

Engineering applies math and science to design, build, test, and improve systems that people rely on—machines, structures, electronics, chemical processes, and more. Engineers balance performance, safety, cost, and sustainability while following standards and often working in cross-functional teams.

Disciplines overlap: mechanical work meets electrical controls; civil projects use environmental and materials knowledge. Strong communication and project management matter as much as technical depth.

Why this field matters to high school students

Engineering pathways reward students who like hands-on projects, physics, and structured problem-solving. Robotics clubs, maker spaces, and math readiness all help you succeed in rigorous college programs.

The field offers broad impact—from clean energy and transportation to medical devices—and many roles pay well, though licensure and advanced study vary by specialty.

Subcategories breakdown

Mechanical engineering

Design and analysis of moving systems—engines, HVAC, robotics, manufacturing equipment—using mechanics, thermodynamics, and materials principles.

Careers in this field

Mechanical engineer, manufacturing engineer, robotics technician (with training path)

Required education level

Bachelor’s in mechanical engineering for engineer titles; PE license may require experience and exams

Salary range (updated)

$65k–$120k+

Civil engineering

Plans and oversees infrastructure—roads, bridges, water systems, and buildings—with attention to safety codes, environmental impact, and long-term durability.

Careers in this field

Civil engineer, structural engineer, transportation planner, survey technician (entry)

Required education level

Bachelor’s; Professional Engineer (PE) licensure common for signing off projects

Salary range (updated)

$60k–$115k+

Electrical engineering

Focuses on circuits, power systems, control systems, and often embedded software—powering everything from grids to consumer electronics.

Careers in this field

Electrical engineer, electronics engineer, control systems engineer

Required education level

Bachelor’s; specialized roles may prefer Master’s

Salary range (updated)

$70k–$130k+

Biomedical engineering

Combines engineering with biology and medicine to develop devices, imaging systems, and therapies that improve health outcomes.

Careers in this field

Biomedical engineer, clinical engineer, R&D specialist in med-tech

Required education level

Bachelor’s entry; research and product R&D often Master’s or PhD

Salary range (updated)

$62k–$125k+
