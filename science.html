Science

Brief overview of the field

Science is a way of learning about the natural world through observation, experiments, and evidence. It spans life sciences (living things), physical sciences (matter and energy), earth and space sciences, and the methods that connect them—hypotheses, data, and peer-reviewed conclusions.

Scientists ask questions, design fair tests, analyze results, and refine models of how reality works. From molecules and cells to ecosystems and galaxies, science builds the knowledge base that medicine, technology, and engineering rely on every day.

Why this field matters to high school students

High school science builds scientific literacy: reading graphs, evaluating claims, and understanding health and environmental issues in the news. Lab work strengthens hands-on skills and teamwork while preparing you for college-level coursework.

Strong science foundations keep doors open to healthcare, research, environmental careers, and technical fields—even if your path later blends with math, engineering, or data.

Subcategories breakdown

Life science (biology)

Study of living organisms—from cells and genetics to physiology, evolution, and ecosystems. Connects to health, agriculture, and conservation.

Careers in this field

Biologist, microbiologist, wildlife technician, lab technologist

Required education level

Bachelor’s for many roles; research and clinical paths often Master’s or PhD

Salary range (updated)

$45k–$95k+ (varies by role and degree)

Chemistry & materials

Explores atoms, molecules, reactions, and materials—from pharmaceuticals and polymers to clean energy and nanotechnology.

Careers in this field

Chemist, materials scientist, quality control analyst, formulation scientist

Required education level

Bachelor’s entry; R&D roles often Master’s or PhD

Salary range (updated)

$50k–$110k+

Earth & environmental science

Focuses on geology, oceans, atmosphere, climate, and human impacts—informing sustainability, resource use, and disaster preparedness.

Careers in this field

Environmental scientist, geologist, hydrologist, GIS specialist

Required education level

Bachelor’s common; advanced roles Master’s

Salary range (updated)

$48k–$100k+

Physics & astronomy

Investigates matter, energy, motion, and the universe—foundational for engineering, medical imaging, semiconductors, and space exploration.

Careers in this field

Physicist, optical engineer (with further study), data analyst, science educator

Required education level

Bachelor’s for analyst/education paths; research physics often PhD

Salary range (updated)

$55k–$130k+ (wide range by specialty)
