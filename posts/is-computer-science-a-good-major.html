Is Computer Science a Good Major for Future STEM Students?

Published by dj21d705bc8c179 · December 4, 2025

Choosing a major can feel overwhelming, especially for middle and high school students just beginning to explore their future careers. If you’re interested in STEM, one major you’ll see everywhere is computer science (CS). But is it actually a good choice?

Here’s a clear, student-friendly breakdown to help you understand what computer science is, what you learn, and why so many people choose it.

What Makes Computer Science a Strong Choice?

A degree with LOTS of job options

Computer science opens the door to a wide range of careers — from software development to cybersecurity, artificial intelligence, data science, game design, and more. Technology is part of every field now, which means CS majors have flexibility when choosing where they want to work.

High earning potential

Careers in technology often offer higher-than-average salaries. According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, many computer and IT jobs pay above the national median wage, making CS a promising long-term investment.

Builds powerful problem-solving skills

In a CS program, you learn how to approach complex problems, think logically, and find solutions through coding, math, and analysis. These skills are valuable even if you choose a different STEM field later on.

A mix of theory and hands-on learning

Students typically study programming, algorithms, data structures, discrete math, databases, and even advanced topics like machine learning or robotics. Along the way, you complete real projects and often gain access to internships that build confidence and experience.

Room to combine interests

Computer science pairs well with many other areas. You can combine CS with design, business, healthcare, engineering, math, or biology and create a unique career path that fits your passions.

When Computer Science Might Not Be the Best Fit

Like any major, CS comes with challenges:

  • It involves math, logic, and detailed problem-solving.
  • Coding requires patience and creativity.
  • If you don’t enjoy working with technology, you may feel frustrated.

Computer science is best for students who enjoy figuring out how things work, solving puzzles, or building digital tools.

What This Means for Middle & High School Students

If you’re still exploring STEM careers, computer science is a great starting point because:

  • You can try beginner coding courses for free online.
  • You’ll learn skills that are useful in business, science, engineering, and technology.
  • You’ll be prepared for careers that continue to grow and evolve.
  • You don’t need to have everything figured out — CS gives you the freedom to explore.
