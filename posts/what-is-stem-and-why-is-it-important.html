What Is STEM and Why Is It Important?

Published by dj21d705bc8c179 · December 4, 2025

Source: BestColleges.com: What Is STEM and Why Is It Important?

Article Summary

This essential article from BestColleges defines STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math) as fields that share a core emphasis on innovation, critical thinking, and problem-solving.

The piece argues that a STEM focus is vital for students today because the U.S. economy suffers from a shortage of qualified STEM professionals—a gap the government and colleges are actively trying to close with funding and incentives.

The article breaks down the various STEM disciplines and highlights the key advantages of pursuing a STEM degree, including:

  • High Demand: STEM careers are predicted to be the “jobs of tomorrow,” remaining in high demand for the foreseeable future.
  • High Income: Many STEM professions offer high salaries straight out of college.
  • Skill Match: The path is ideal for students who excel at math and enjoy working with new technology or prefer computer-based roles.

For students exploring their path, this article provides a helpful table of popular STEM careers, including Software Developers and Mathematicians/Statisticians, along with their salaries and impressive projected job growth rates.
