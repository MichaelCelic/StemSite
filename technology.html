Technology

Brief overview of the field

Technology applies scientific and mathematical knowledge to design, build, and maintain tools, systems, and digital products—from software and networks to hardware and user-facing apps. It blends problem-solving with creativity as teams iterate, test, and ship reliable solutions.

The field evolves quickly: new languages, frameworks, security threats, and data tools appear often, so curiosity and continuous learning are central to long-term success.

Why this field matters to high school students

Digital skills are now baseline for most careers. Understanding how computers, data, and the internet work helps you study more effectively, collaborate online, and protect your privacy.

Early exposure to coding, IT basics, or design can clarify whether you enjoy building products, securing systems, or analyzing data—and it strengthens applications for internships and college programs.

Subcategories breakdown

Software & computing

Design and development of applications, systems software, and APIs—using programming languages, version control, testing, and deployment practices.

Careers in this field

Software developer, mobile app developer, backend engineer, QA engineer

Required education level

Bachelor’s common; strong portfolios and bootcamps can supplement degrees

Salary range (updated)

$65k–$150k+ (varies by region and level)

Cybersecurity & IT

Protecting systems and data from attacks; managing networks, identity, and incident response; aligning technology with policy and risk.

Careers in this field

Security analyst, network administrator, IT support specialist, penetration tester

Required education level

Associate / Bachelor’s; certifications (e.g. CompTIA, vendor certs) often valued

Salary range (updated)

$50k–$130k+

Data & analytics

Collecting, cleaning, modeling, and visualizing data to support decisions—spanning databases, statistics, and increasingly machine learning workflows.

Careers in this field

Data analyst, database administrator, business intelligence analyst, data engineer (entry paths vary)

Required education level

Bachelor’s typical; advanced analytics roles may prefer Master’s

Salary range (updated)

$55k–$125k+

Digital design & product

User experience (UX), user interface (UI), and product thinking—making technology accessible, usable, and aligned with real user needs.

Careers in this field

UX designer, UI designer, product designer, technical writer

Required education level

Bachelor’s or design bootcamp + portfolio; many paths exist

Salary range (updated)

$50k–$120k+
