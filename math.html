Math

Brief overview of the field

Mathematics studies numbers and patterns and mathematical structures and logical connections between things. The subject encompasses various mathematical fields which include algebra and geometry and calculus and statistics and probability. Math involves solving problems to study how various quantities interact with each other. Mathematics establishes the basic principles which we use to understand our surroundings through basic arithmetic and complex mathematical equations that simulate actual systems.

Why this field matters to high school students

High school students need to study mathematics because it develops vital abilities that include critical thinking and problem-solving and logical reasoning. The skills mentioned above serve both academic purposes and practical needs because they help people manage their finances and interpret news statistics and make informed choices.

Subcategories breakdown

Applied Mathematics

Mathematical theories and techniques to solve real-world problems across engineering, science, finance, and technology.

Careers in this field

Mathematician, financial analyst, and operations analyst

Required education level

Bachelor’s and Master’s

Salary range (updated)

$70k–$90k+

Statistics

Statistics involves the collection and organization of numerical data which researchers use to analyze patterns and draw conclusions for decision-making under uncertain conditions.

Careers in this field

Accountant, data analyst, and statistician

Required education level

Bachelor’s and Master’s

Salary range (updated)

$60k–$100k+

Algebra & calculus foundations

Core math that models change, rates, and relationships—essential for physics, engineering, economics, and advanced STEM. Students build fluency with functions, limits, derivatives, and integrals that unlock higher-level science and tech courses.

Careers in this field

Actuary, quantitative analyst, secondary math educator

Required education level

Associate / Bachelor’s (foundations); advanced roles often Master’s

Salary range (updated)

$55k–$120k+ (varies widely by role)
